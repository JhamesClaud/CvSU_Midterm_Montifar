Cavite State University-General Trias

Mission

Cavite State University shall provide excellent, equitable and relevant educational opportunities in the arts, sciences and technology through quality instruction and responsive research and development activities. It shall produce professional, skilled and morally upright individuals for global competitiveness

Vission

The premier university in historic Cavite globally recognized for excellence in character development, academics, research, innovation and sustainable community engagement.

Core Values

Truth

Exellence

Service

Quality Policy

We Commit to the highest standards of education, value our stakeholders, Strive for continual improvement of our products and services, and Uphold the University's tenets of Truth, Excellence, and Service to produce globally competitive and morally upright individuals.

General Rules and Regulations

STUDENT CLASSIFICATION

A FULL-TIME STUDENT - one who is registered for formal academic credits - carries the full load for a given semester
A PART-TIME STUDENT - one who is registered for formal credits - carries less than the full load for a given semester
A TRANSFER STUDENT - one who comes from another college/ university where he started studying for a course. - who is registered in the University after fulfilling all requirements as transfer students.
CURRICULAR CLASSIFICATION - shall be based on the actual number of academic units completed as required for particular curricular year. - FRESHMAN, SOPHOMORE, JUNIOR & SENIOR

SHIFTING TO OTHER PROGRAM

- students who intend to shift to another program must accomplish a prescribed form for the purpose be approved by the Dean of the College where they want to shift to.
- students pursuing degree program may be allowed to shift to non-degree programs upon approval of their application for shifting.
- students pursuing non-degree program may be allowed to shift to degree program offered by a college after satisfying the ff. requirements:
a. GPA of 2.0 or better
b. submission of approved application form and other supporting documents
c. passing the required entrance examination * EDUCATION - GPA of 2.25 or better ECONOMICS/ - GPA of 2.25 or better MANAGEMENT

CROSS REGISTRATION

- should have a written permission from their school registrar to be presented to the CVSU Registrar.
- permits shall state the subject(s) and the total number of units the students is allowed to cross register and that the University shall be that venue from the course to be registered.
- for courses w/ pre-requisites they should present an authenticated proof of equivalency of course & description.

LATE REGISTRATION

-The period for late registration shall be seven school days after the regular registration schedule.
-No late registrants will be entertained after this period.
-Undergraduate and non-degree students who register during the authorized period for the late registration shall be charged a fine of P100.00.
-No late registration shall be entertained for the summer program.

REFUND OF FEES

- students who withdraw their registration from the University after a period of not more than 3 weeks or fifteen days of regular classes shall be refundable.

ACADEMIC LOAD

- no students shall be allowed to take more than the maximum credit units per semester.
- a graduating student may be allowed to enroll more than the maximum credit units not to exceed 26 units during the last semester.

CLASS ATTENDANCE

- a student who has been absent from classes at least 2 consecutive meetings must obtain an excuse slip from the Office of Student Affairs.
- excuses are for time missed only.
- if a student has been absent in 20% of the time schedule devoted to the class without justification/reasons he/she shall be dropped from the rolls.

CHANGING OF SECTION AND DROPPING OF COURSES

-transfer to other courses or to other sections must be made for valid reasons such as: the course is not needed, advised, and conflict in schedule.
-no change in matriculation will be allowed after 3 weeks of regular classes.
-Change in subject can be accomplished by filling up a changing/dropping form duly noted by the Department Chair and the instructor concerned and approved by the Campus Registrar and Campus Dean.
A student shall be notified by the College University Registrar to drop a course subject to the ff. circumstances:
1.) Registered higher course w/out passing the pre-requisite course(s); and
2.) Registered major course w/out passing all the required basic courses except in cases where the basic courses are offered in a semester concurrent with the major course.

RE-ENROLLMENT OF SUBJECTS.

- no students shall be allowed to repeat or re-enroll a subject for more than 3x.
- a students who fails a subject for the 3x shall be permanently disqualified from further registration in the University.

PRE-REQUISITE SUBJECTS

- a student shall not be allowed to register an advanced subject w/o passing/satisfying the prerequisite subject(s) specified in the curriculum.

LEAVE OF ABSENCE

- a student who is granted leave of absence (LOA) w/in 75% of the time devoted to a semester/term.

HONORABLE DISMISSAL

- shall be issued by the University Registrar to a student who stopped schooling in the University provided that he was not found guilty or misdemeanor under the University Student’s Norm of Conduct.

REMOVAL/COMPLETION OF GRADES OF “4.0” AND “Inc.”
- a grade of “4.0” and “Inc.” may be removed through anyone of the ff:

1.) Without paying the Special examination Fee - a grade of “4.0” may be removed within one year by taking the examination during the regular schedule for removal examinations. - an “Inc.” grade may be removed by completing the requirements for the subject anytime within one (1) year provided he/she is currently enrolled.
2.) Paying the Special Examination Fee - a grade of “4.0” may be removed by a special removal examination administered anytime upon payment of special examination of P10.00/unit

RULES IN SCHOLASTIC DELIQUENCY

Types of Academic Deficiency
1. Warning: Any student who at the end of the sem, is found to have dropped the course or obtain INC or failing grades or combination thereof, in 30% or 50% of the enrolled subjects.
Warning status for two consecutive semesters place the student on the probationary status in the succeeding sem.
2. Probation: any student who at the end of the sem, is found to have dropped the course or obtain INC, or grades below “3.0” or combination thereof in 51% or more of the enrolled subjects. Academic load of students shall be limited to 15 units only.
If he/she incurs probationary status for two consecutive semesters, he/she will be classified under disqualification status in the next sem.
3. Disqualification: any student who at the end of the sem fails in at least 75% or more of the enrolled subjects. He/she shall be ineligible to enroll for one sem. He/she shall be allowed to carry an academic load of not more than 15 units when he enrolls in the succeeding sem.
1).A student shall only be allowed two disqualification status after which he shall no longer be eligible to continue his studies in the University.
2).Student who has 24 units or fewer academic units remaining in their curriculum will be allowed to re-enroll despite reaching the academic deficiency limits.
3).No readmission of disqualified students shall be considered by the Office of the University Registrar w/o favorable recommendation of the Readmission Committee.
3).The responsibility for evaluation of academic deficiencies still lies with the students.
5).The office of the Campus Registrar/ Department Chair/ Registration Advisers shall notify the student of his/her academic deficiency status every end of the semester or before enrollment.

TIME LIMIT RULE

Degree students (4-year course) shall be required to finish their studies within, at most, two (2) years beyond the prescribed number of years.
Non-degree (2-year course) shall be required to finish their diplomas within, at most, one year beyond the prescribed number of years in the curricula.
